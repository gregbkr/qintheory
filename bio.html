Charlotte Qin | 秦 超 穎

Charlotte Qin is a Chinese-Canadian ArtScientist based in Geneva. Trained as a physicist and a designer, her creation is motivated and guided by her curiosity and love for nature.

work with me

River Arve in Geneva, 2018

Charlotte was born and raised in the mountainous Anhui province in China and moved to Toronto, Canada with her family at the age of 15. She received her BSc in Physics from McGill University, MA at the Royal College of Art and MSc at Imperial College London in Innovation Design Engineering. Since the winter of 2020, she has been residing in Geneva, Switzerland sprouting her professional life.

Throughout her early 20s, she left Canada multiple times to pursue her scientific and artistic education in the world. She had studied at the University of Geneva as an exchange student, following her summer internship with the ATLAS Experiment of CERN in 2014. After her bachelor's study in physics in 2017, she had stepped on her truth-seeking journey in the inner world and completed her first 10-day Vipassana meditation course in Montbello, Quebec. She sees the dance of art and science in her curiosity and creation as the embodiment of her cultural fluidity between the mythical Chinese and the western-scientific way.

During her Master's study between 2018 and 2019, she had actively involved herself working in collaboration with scientists and scientific institutes as an artist and designer. These friendships and partnerships include Imperial College London, the University of Edinburgh, and Bell Labs in the UK, as well as CERN, Campus Biotech in Geneva, Switzerland.
